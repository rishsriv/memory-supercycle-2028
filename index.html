Memory prices to 2028, corrected for bit density

A per-kilogram price proxy conflates three things: the real price per bit, rising chip density (more bits per kg every year), and product mix. Stripping out density with Korea customs data cuts the apparent price surge roughly in half — but what remains is still one of the sharpest memory upcycles on record, and a genuine break from decades of falling quality-adjusted prices.

Methodology & assumptions

The identity. Export value = tonnage × (bits per kg) × (price per bit). So $/kg = density × price/bit, and price/bit ≈ ($/kg) ÷ density_index. Korea customs gives value and weight directly (confirmed: these HS lines carry only US$ and kg — no piece count), so tonnage and $/kg need no assumptions. Only the split of $/kg into density vs price/bit requires an assumed density-growth rate.

Density assumptions (bits per kg, annual). DRAM +17%/yr (scaling slowed at 1α–1γ nodes); NAND +28%/yr (3D layer stacking still compounds); HBM/multichip +12%/yr (capacity per stack rises but packaging weight rises with it). These are deliberately round, defensible figures — not vendor-published.

Sensitivity. Results move with the density rate but the qualitative story is robust. DRAM's real price/bit off its trough is 7.1× at 12%/yr, 6.1× at 17%, 5.3× at 22%, 4.6× at 27% — a large real rise under any reasonable assumption. NAND/HBM landing near their 2020 real levels holds across the range.

Caveats. Unit value still embeds product mix within an HS line (e.g. DDR5/server share, HBM stack height) that density alone doesn't capture. BLS import/PPI indices are matched-model and density-neutral but cover broad NAICS 3344 (all electronic components), so they understate memory-specific moves — treat them as the conservative bound. The 2028 scenarios are an analyst synthesis anchored to guidance that extends only "through 2027." Latest underlying data: May 2026.

How this was built. Four parallel FactIQ research agents pulled Korea Customs export value + weight by HS code (DRAM 8542321010, NAND/flash 8542321030, multichip/HBM 85423240xx), BLS import-price & PPI indices (incl. 60-year history), Micron/Nvidia SEC financials, and forward company guidance; the density decomposition and scenarios were computed on top.
