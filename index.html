How this was built. Four parallel research agents queried FactIQ: (1) Korea Customs Service export data by HS code — DRAM (8542321010), NAND/flash (8542321030) and multichip/HBM modules (85423240xx) — to compute export unit value (US$/kg) as a price proxy; (2) BLS import-price & PPI indices for semiconductors; (3) Micron & Nvidia SEC-filed quarterly financials plus China/Taiwan customs; (4) forward company guidance from earnings coverage.

Caveats. Unit value (value÷weight) is a directional price proxy, not a contract price — it embeds product-mix shifts, so absolute US$/kg levels overstate like-for-like chip price moves; treat the shape and magnitude of change as the signal. BLS publishes no standalone DRAM/NAND index, so the Asian-NIC import series is the closest official proxy. The 2028 scenarios are an analyst synthesis anchored to company guidance (tightness confirmed only "through 2027"); no memory maker has issued 2028 pricing guidance. Latest FactIQ data: May 2026.
